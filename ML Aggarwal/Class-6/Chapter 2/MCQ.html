Question 1. The smallest whole number is:

(a) 0
(b) 1
(c) 10
(d) 100

Answer:

(a) 0

Question 2. How many whole numbers are there between 21 and 61?

(a) 39
(b) 40
(c) 41
(d) 42

Answer:

Total whole numbers between 21 and 61 = (61 – 21) – 1 = 40 – 1 = 39
(a) 39

Question 3. Successor of 999 is:

(a) 998
(b) 1000
(c) 9999
(d) none of these

Answer:

Successor of 999 = 999 + 1 = 1000
(b) 1000

Question 4. Predecessor of 1000 is:

(a) 999
(b) 1001
(c) 9999
(d) none of these

Answer:

Predecessor of 1000 = 1000 – 1 = 999
(a) 999

Question 5. The product of non-zero whole number and its successor is:

(a) always an even number
(b) always an odd number
(c) even or odd
(d) none of these

Answer:

The product of a non-zero whole number and its successor is always an even number.
(a) always an even number

Question 6. The sum of two odd numbers is:

(a) an odd number
(b) an even number
(c) sometimes even sometimes odd
(d) none of these

Answer:

The sum of two odd numbers is an even number.
(b) an even number

Question 7. The sum of an even number and an odd number is:

(a) an odd number
(b) an even number
(c) sometimes even sometimes odd
(d) none of these

Answer:

The sum of an even number and an odd number is an odd number.
(a) an odd number

Question 8. The multiplicative identity for whole numbers is:

(a) 0
(b) 1
(c) any number
(d) none of these

Answer:

The multiplicative identity for whole numbers is 1.
(b) 1

Question 9. The additive identity for whole numbers is:

(a) 0
(b) 1
(c) any number
(d) none of these

Answer:

The additive identity for whole numbers is 0.
(a) 0

Question 10. The value of 25 × 36 × 4 is:

(a) 3600
(b) 360
(c) 36
(d) 36000

Answer:

25 × 36 × 4 = (25 × 4) × 36 = 100 × 36 = 3600
(a) 3600
